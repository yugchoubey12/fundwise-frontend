Taxation

How much tax you'll actually pay. India-specific, Gen-Z friendly.

When you make profit from mutual funds, you need to pay tax on those gains. Here's what Gen-Z needs to know:

Equity Mutual Funds

Holding Period (Short-term) Less than 1 year
Short-term Tax Rate 15%
Holding Period (Long-term) More than 1 year
Long-term Tax Rate 10% (above ₹1L gains)

Debt Mutual Funds

Tax Treatment As per income slab
For 20% bracket 20% tax
For 30% bracket 30% tax
Indexation Benefit Not available
Simple Example:

You invested ₹1,00,000 in an equity fund and sold it after 2 years for ₹1,50,000. Your profit is ₹50,000.

Since you held it for more than 1 year, it's long-term. First ₹1 lakh of gains is tax-free. Your ₹50,000 profit is below that, so you pay ZERO tax!

✓ What Gen-Z Should Remember

  • Hold equity funds for at least 1 year to benefit from lower long-term tax rates
  • First ₹1 lakh of long-term equity gains per year is tax-free
  • Debt fund taxation is less favorable—gains taxed as per your income slab
  • ELSS funds offer tax deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C (but lock-in period is 3 years)
  • Always consult a tax advisor for your specific situation

Tax rules can change. Always stay updated or consult a professional before making decisions based on tax implications.
