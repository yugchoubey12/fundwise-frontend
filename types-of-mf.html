Types of Mutual Funds

Equity, debt, hybrid—which one's right for you?

There are different types of mutual funds based on what they invest in and their risk level. Here's a simple breakdown:

Equity Funds

High Risk

Invest primarily in stocks (equities). Higher potential returns but more ups and downs.

Best for: Long-term goals (5+ years)
Example returns: 12-15% p.a.
Who should invest: Young investors with time

Debt Funds

Low Risk

Invest in bonds and fixed-income securities. More stable with lower but predictable returns.

Best for: Short to medium-term (1-3 years)
Example returns: 6-8% p.a.
Who should invest: Risk-averse investors

Hybrid Funds

Medium Risk

Mix of both equity and debt. Balanced approach for moderate risk and returns.

Best for: Medium-term goals (3-5 years)
Example returns: 9-12% p.a.
Who should invest: Moderate risk takers

Index Funds

Medium Risk

Track a market index like NIFTY 50. Low cost, passive management, market returns.

Best for: Long-term passive investors
Example returns: 10-12% p.a.
Who should invest: Beginners wanting market returns
