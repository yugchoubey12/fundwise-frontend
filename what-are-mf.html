What Are Mutual Funds?

The basics, explained in 5 minutes. No finance degree needed.

A mutual fund is like a group investment plan where you and thousands of other people pool your money together. A professional fund manager then invests this pooled money in stocks, bonds, or other securities on behalf of everyone.

üçï Think of it like ordering pizza with friends

Instead of buying a whole pizza alone (expensive!), you split the cost with friends. Everyone gets a slice, and you all enjoy the variety. Similarly, with mutual funds, you pool money with others to buy a diversified portfolio of investments that you couldn't afford alone.

When you invest in a mutual fund, you buy "units" of the fund. Each unit represents your share of ownership in the fund's portfolio. As the investments grow in value, so does the value of your units.

  • Diversification: Your money is spread across multiple stocks/bonds
  • Low investment: Start with as little as ‚Çπ500
  • Liquidity: Easy to buy and sell (for open-ended funds)
